Assumptions & methodology

Source: public SEC filings & wage data

  1. Chipotle FY2025 10-K (period ended Dec 31, 2025): 4,056 restaurants; labor cost of $2,991,680K; labor at 25.1% of revenue. SEC EDGAR.
  2. Chipotle FY2024 10-K: 130,504 employees worldwide (126,233 in-restaurant) as of Dec 31, 2024 — most recent filing with precise headcount. SEC EDGAR.
  3. Cava Group FY2025 10-K (period ended Dec 28, 2025): ~12,900 restaurant team members across 439 restaurants; FY2025 revenue $1,179.7M; labor ~25.8% of revenue.
  4. 44,000+ fast casual units excludes QSRs (Technomic Top 500, 2025).
  5. NY State minimum wage, effective Jan 1, 2026: $17.00/hr downstate NYC, no tip credit for fast food chains (NY DOL).

Company analysis — not sourced to filings

  1. $20.00/hr NYC market wage: above the $17.00 legal floor, reflecting competitive NYC fast-casual hiring — a modeling choice, not filed data.
  2. Payroll taxes & insurance, ~12% of wages: FICA (statutory) plus NY SUI, FUTA, MTA payroll tax, workers' comp, and DBL — an estimated composite, not itemized in any filing.
  3. OT / spread-of-hours premium, ~3% of wages: NY spread-of-hours rule, estimated incidence.
  4. Benefits, meals & uniforms, ~$2.15/hr: estimated partial benefit take-up typical of fast-casual crews, not company-specific.
  5. Turnover cost, amortized, ~$1.70/hr: assumes ~120%+ annual churn (industry-typical, not Chipotle/Cava-specific) × ~$3,000 replacement cost per hire — both industry rules of thumb, not sourced to a filing.
  6. Compliance & scheduling admin, ~$0.50/hr: NYC Fair Workweek overhead estimate.
  7. Coverage hours for "per cashier position": 60 hrs/wk peak-only, or 91 hrs/wk open-to-close — an operational assumption, not derived from any filing.
  8. *Checkout time and AI accuracy are estimates based on internal testing, not an audited multi-location result.
  9. †"Zero cashier labor" applies to card and contactless transactions today; cash currently routes through your existing POS with staff input.
  10. 99% AI accuracy is a target assumption, not an audited multi-location result.
  11. Hardware is leased (included in the all-in monthly fee), not a capital purchase; there is no upfront hardware cost to the operator. Net savings and return-on-cost depend on location volume, staffing, wage, and operating hours.
  12. Card and contactless transactions avoid dedicated cashier input; cash may require staff input through your existing POS.
  13. ‡Chargeback economics: processors charge merchants a non-refundable fee per dispute — commonly $15–$50, and up to ~$100 for high-risk accounts — on top of the lost sale and staff time (Mastercard 2025 State of Chargebacks; Investopedia; Chargebacks911, 2026). At an assumed ~1 disputed transaction per 1,000 orders, a 250-bowl/day location clears ~91,000 card orders/yr, so even a handful of disputes eliminated per month recovers hundreds of dollars annually in fees alone — before counting recovered sale value. Dispute reduction is an expected benefit of a timestamped, itemized visual record tied to each charge — not yet measured across live pilots.
